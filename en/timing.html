Action Timing

The code has no magic hour. But three mechanisms make timing matter — and the clock below computes your audience's active windows from your posting language.

Why timing matters: three code mechanisms

  1. The 30-minute half-life is a speed race. A post's real-time buzz features lose half their power every 30 minutes (TimelinesOnlineAggregationConfigBase.scala). You need engagement density right after posting. If your readers are asleep, the speed never builds.
  2. Ranking is a relative contest at every refresh. Your post competes against ~1,500 candidates each time a reader refreshes. Posting when your target readers are active = entering the most contests.
  3. The first wave of engagement locks the direction. The system tracks each post's engagement per country in real time (TweetCountryEngagementRealTimeAggregateFeatureHydrator.scala), and each liker's interest vector gets added to the post's profile (SimClusters). Whoever engages in the first hour decides which countries and communities see the post next. Post in English during Asian daytime hours and the first wave comes from the wrong audience — pulling distribution the wrong way.
⚠️ The takeaway: posting time is how you choose your first engagers
It's not just "post when people are online" — it's "post when your target audience is awake and everyone else is asleep." The overlap window is the sweet spot. That's exactly what the clock below computes.

How it's computed — and its limits

  • What comes from the code: the three mechanisms above are source-code facts. The "what hour" part is not — X has not open-sourced per-country activity curves.
  • What this tool estimates: ① audience timezone weights per language are rough estimates (shown under the clock — adjust against your own analytics); ② the activity curve is a generic social-media model (morning ramp, lunch bump, evening prime time), not official X data; ③ timezone math uses the browser's Intl API live, so daylight saving is handled automatically.
  • How to calibrate: treat the "best/second-best" windows as a starting point. Post 5–6 comparable pieces in each window over two weeks and let your own impression data pick the real winner — your actual follower mix is the final authority.
