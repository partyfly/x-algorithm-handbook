WeChat Official Account Playbook

WeChat doesn't open-source its algorithm. But its traffic strategy can be reverse-engineered from three official mirrors: the analytics metrics it exposes (what it measures), the operation rules (what it punishes), and founder statements (what it wants). Every claim below carries an evidence grade.

⚠️ About the evidence: unlike the X handbook, this page reverse-engineers a closed system. Four grades: Official = written rules; Doctrine = Allen Zhang / WeChat Open Class statements; Metrics = data dimensions the analytics dashboard exposes (what's measured ≈ what's optimized); Third-party = operator-community observations, unconfirmed — treat as rumor. All specific multipliers floating around (e.g. "1.3× boost for read-through") are third-party; this page trusts directions, never numbers.

Method

  • Dashboard metrics = the algorithm's recipe. Whatever data the platform shows you (read-completion rate, regular readers, "Wow" taps, share cascades) is what it measures — and what it measures is what it optimizes.
  • The penalty list = the algorithm's negative weights. The Operation Rules and Recommendation Guidelines spell out, item by item, what won't be recommended and what gets banned — more reliable than any leak.
  • Founder statements = the objective function. Allen Zhang's "1:2:10" and "social recommendation is the most human way to discover information" give the system's long-term direction.

The three-layer funnel

WeChat = subscriptions as the base + social graph as the amplifier + machine recommendations as the ceiling. "Light on subscriptions, heavy on recommendations" is the stated direction.

Doctrine Allen Zhang, 2019: "social recommendation is the most human way to get information"; in 2021 he wrote the target ratio 1 : 2 : 10 (following : friends : machine).

Official The "Wow" tap evolved into a standalone Friends♡ entry, placed before subscribed content in the feed; the team keeps expanding the test.

Evidence 36Kr's small account "36Kr Future Consumption": recommendation traffic grew from 24.3% (2023-08) to 45.9% (2025-03) of total reads; their flagship account sits at just 2.2% — the smaller the account, the bigger the recommendation share.

✅ The one-line takeaway
The Official Account platform is no longer a subscription medium. For small accounts, about half the traffic comes from recommendations — your real arena is the rec pool and the Friends♡ social chain. Follower count is just the foundation.

Playbook ① Per-article checklist

  1. Three-switch check: originality declared ✓ full broadcast (no segments) ✓ "don't recommend" unchecked ✓ — miss one and recs are zero
  2. Restrained titles: summarize accurately; no shock, no commands, no cliffhanger bait. Clickbait here is a ban risk, not a growth hack
  3. Structure for read-through: open with the article's value promise; section headers + figures + bolded takeaways; long-form ≠ rambling
  4. Close with a natural "Wow" prompt (no rewards): Wow feeds the Friends♡ stream. Never "share to Moments for a gift" — that's the 30-day-ban line
  5. No redirect bait in the body: QR codes, "click for the full version", third-party funnels — rule 5.4 zeroes your eligibility
  6. No first-hour vigil needed: the rec cycle runs 3–7 days. Spend that hour writing tomorrow's piece instead

Playbook ② Starting a new account (the opposite of X)

  • No cold-start purgatory: the rec pool is open to new accounts (36Kr's small-account data + consistent third-party reports). Write your first piece at full quality and aim straight for recommendations
  • First 10 articles: all original, all in one niche — that's both your eligibility and the fastest way to converge the system's content tags onto your domain
  • Run the official health check: Dashboard → Account Growth → Account Check — official self-audit across compliance / rec guidelines / account status / traffic feedback. This is WeChat's closest thing to "source code"; run it monthly
  • Never buy followers: fake reads are visible in completion curves (real readers exit at the end; bots exit at the top), and follower count doesn't buy rec traffic anyway — small accounts get a higher rec share
  • Watch the rec share: in read sources, a rising "recommended + Top Stories" share = your content clears the bar; stuck at zero = check the three switches, then the not-recommended list

Playbook ③ Building regular readers

  • Fixed publishing time (e.g. 18:30 daily): "regular reader" status is a habit, and habits form on rhythm — this is WeChat's version of recharging edge weights
  • Run named columns: a recurring series builds expectations ("one source-code teardown every Monday" beats random topics)
  • Track weekly regular-reader change, not follower count: regulars up = relationships thickening; followers up with flat regulars = you're collecting tourists
  • Frequency serves quality: third-party claims high frequency dilutes per-article recs; true or not, WeChat has no X-style daily-presence pressure — one strong piece a day beats three fillers, even more so here

Playbook ④ Review cadence

  • Per article: check on day 3 and day 7 (long rec cycle; day-1 numbers don't decide anything). Four numbers: delivery-open rate (title), read-completion (content), Wow rate (spread), rec share (breakout)
  • Per account: monthly: regular-reader trend + the official health check + read-source structure
  • Diagnosis tree: low open rate → fix titles/covers; low completion → fix structure/length; low Wow → ending lacks resonance; zero rec share → check the three switches and the violation list

FAQ

Q: Does prompting readers to tap "Wow" count as inducement?

The inducement rules target rewarded/forced sharing to Moments. A natural, reward-free "Wow" prompt at the end is standard practice and not on the punishment list. The red line is the exchange of benefits: any "tap/share to receive X" falls under inducement.

Q: Will AI-assisted writing get throttled?

What's targeted is "AIGC misinformation" and "one-click AI sludge" — the keywords are false and low-quality, not AI itself. AI assistance + human fact-checking + real informational value is fine. Pure AI mass-production walks into both the low-quality and misinformation lines.

Q: Does publish time still matter?

Its weight is falling: rec distribution is decoupled from push time (3–7 day cycle). But subscription opens and Wow cascades still have an evening peak, and fixed timing builds the regular-reader habit — keep a fixed slot, stop worrying about it.

Q: Is reposting good articles worth anything?

Official and explicit: reposted content is never recommended. Reposts only collect subscription and forward traffic — near zero for small accounts. For curation, write an original commentary that quotes, instead of whitelist-reposting.

Q: Why is my recommendation traffic zero?

Check in order: ① the three switches (original / full broadcast / rec allowed) → ② the official health check → ③ whether the content hits the not-recommended list (redirect bait / marketing / clickbait / stale) → ④ whether read-completion is too low. The first three are eligibility — binary; only the fourth means "the content isn't good enough yet."
